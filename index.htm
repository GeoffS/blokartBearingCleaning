The process is really very easy, but if you want to keep your hands from getting attacked by the chemicals and grease, I'll be happy to sell you completely cleaned and packed bearings: for $20/pair (I also sell them cleaned and ready to lubricate with your own concoction).

Cleaning:

Like almost all blokart® racers I've tried all kinds of lubricants in my bearings.

I've also tried all kinds of ways to get the OEM grease out of them.
Many of the techniques probably weren't very good for the bearings (ex. spinning at high-RPM with compressed air when they weren't lubricated).
I was about to purchase an ultrasonic cleaner when I discovered that assembled ball-bearings can be damaged by the vibrations.

I set out to develop a cleaning system that was:

  1. Easy to do at home.
  2. Didn't use any really nasty chemicals.
  3. Resonably quick and easy.
  4. Didn't spin the bearings when they weren't lubricated (or damage them in any other way).

After doing a lot of research on the internet, I decided to use an industrial, water-based de-greaser at elevated temperature.
The specific cleaner I choose was ZEP Purple Degreaser. I could get it at my local Menards home-center and it got high praise on various mechanic's and machining web-sites.
